My name is Alexander Ulyanov, and this humble website is where I publish a few notes, both in Russian and in English.

I am 27; I was born in Yekaterinburg (about the middle of Russia), studied English and Linguistics in college (not for long), and moved into St. Petersburg in 2011. St. Petersburg, Leninski Avenue is where I happen to live at the moment with my significant other. I love St. Petersburg. It is large, and it has sea, and it is the only Russian city (apart from Moscow maybe) which does not insult my aesthetic sense.

I love local history, and, to the point of geekdom, topography, toponymy, and road infrastructure. I know entire St. Petersburg by heart, and this is almost not an exagerration. I love travel, especially by car, and Northern Europe, although I can enjoy Russian backwater as well. I'm obsessed with Nordic countries, particularly Finland and their northermost parts (Lapland). I like stories and movies where evil prevails, or at least some really dark and/or antisocial good guys. I like unhealthy food, raccoons and rabbits, being a smartass, taking photos of places of interest, road trips on my Renault Sandero or walking around the city, procrastinating with my laptop and drinking beer, pretty much whatever everyone does. I have very liberal views, and as Dostoyevsky said, that always makes one somewhat of a Russophobe. Nonetheless I'm of fairly high opinion about the humanity in general, and I believe there are nowhere as many assholes in the world as it is commonly believed. This still doesn't prevent me from being as asocial as befits a neckbeard geek. Although I have no beard.

I work as a Senior Developer in Propeller Ads online advertising company. I'm a full stack web developer, specializing on the backend, with modern PHP and Symfony 2 web framework. I have loads of expertise with Symfony, particularly in the area of legacy code integration, which is what I tried to write about here before I got bored. I like Java and C++ a lot, but I only have small real-world experience with them. I really dislike frontend and Javascript, although with AngularJS it becomes at least tolerable. Some of my old code is available at GitHub, but most of what I develop is not publicly available.

I have always had difficulties with publishing anything and especially keeping it up-to-date on the web, but this website (on GitHub pages) is really small and simple, so I hope it won't be quite as bad.
